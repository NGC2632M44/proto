# On the Name "Proto" — The First Glue

**Proto** is named directly from the classical etymology of "Protocol," marking its foundational role in the on-device intelligence stack.

## Etymology

"Protocol" derives from Ancient Greek **prōtokollon** (πρωτόκολλον), a compound of two roots:

- **prōtos (πρῶτος)** — "first."
- **kolla (κόλλα)** — "glue."

## Original Metaphor & System Mapping

In classical antiquity, *prōtokollon* denoted the flyleaf pasted onto the front of a papyrus scroll. This page carried no body text. It provided the scroll's official certificate of authenticity, a content summary, and the parsing contract. It was the system's "first drop of glue" — without it, everything that followed lost its legitimacy and its reading baseline.

In the current engineering architecture, **Proto** plays the identical role. It does not execute business logic (the "body text," or Skill). It is the **a priori contract** by which the system distills, parses, and seals high-entropy, unstructured engineering context (the Harness) into low-entropy, on-device automation. **Proto is the first drop of glue between chaotic context and deterministic action.**

This naming marks the project not as a single tool, but as the **underlying grammar and infrastructure** of on-device intelligent automation. Every subsequently derived Skill instance and SOP orchestration is founded upon this contract.
